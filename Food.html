It's Almost the Holiday Season!

It's almost the holiday season! During the holidays, families and friends tend to gather in one place, around the dining room table. We talk and celebrate special events, catch up on each other's lives, and lastly, we eat the delicious food we prepare with our families! With holidays come traditions—dyeing Easter eggs, exchanging wrapped gifts, carving pumpkins—and many of our holiday traditions center around food. Think about it. What's Thanksgiving without a turkey?

Anyway, if you're looking for something to prepare on the Holidays, this list is for you!

1.) Pumpkin Pie

One of the common foods in Halloween!


Traditional pumpkin pie is a true classic. Its rustic orange color and sweet, earthy taste make it a favorite for fall celebrations. Historians believe the first pumpkin pies—or at least a forerunner to pumpkin pie — were made by American settlers at Plymouth.

The Native Americans showed the Europeans many uses for pumpkin. More like a custard than an actual pie, the Pilgrims filled a hollowed out pumpkin with milk, honey, and spices, then baked it in hot ashes on the fire. In 1796, Amelia Simmons wrote the first cookbook ever created and published in America.

This cookbook, "American Cookery," featured probably the first real pumpkin pie recipe -- though she called it "pumpkin pudding." Although you can make a pumpkin pie from scratch, many people choose to utilize canned pumpkin for the task. Canned pumpkin appeared on grocery shelves starting in 1929 and remains a pantry staple to this day.

2.) Eggnog

A tasty Christmas Drink!



Talk about a classic holiday meal. Have you ever had eggnog in September or March? You can't even find it on the grocery store shelves if it's not the Christmas season.

The literal meaning of eggnog is "egg inside a small cup." It's a drink traditionally served at holiday get-togethers and is typically alcoholic (although you can make or purchase nonalcoholic versions). The rich beverage contains eggs, sugar, milk, and some sort of alcohol.

Eggnog evolved from a hot British drink known as "posset," consisting of eggs, milk, and ale or wine. Depending on the locale, the amount of alcohol in the drink varies. In the South, most people drink their eggnog with bourbon. In other places, you might find eggnog with rum or brandy.

Today it's so ubiquitous, you can find all sorts of eggnog — dairy-free, soy-based, fat-free, you name it!

3.) Christmas Ham

A Filipino Family Favorite!



In the Philippines and among diaspora groups such as Filipino-Americans, a ham is prepared for Christmas celebrations. The ham is referred to as a hamón, from the Spanish word for "ham", jamón. The hamón is sometimes soaked in kumquat and lime juice.

It is similar to, but not to be confused for, hamonado, which is another Filipino dish that can include pork and is also served around the Christmas season.

4.) Macaroni Salad

Delicious and Easy to Make!




Macaroni salad is quite easy and very common to make in the Philippines.It is a type of pasta salad, served cold made with cooked elbow macaroni and usually prepared with mayonnaise.

It is very common in Filipino households when it is Christmas, and it is often served as a side dish to barbecue, fried chicken, and other picnic style dishes.

Like any dish, national and regional variations abound but generally it is prepared with raw diced onions, dill or sweet pickles and celery and seasoned with salt and pepper.

5.) Spaghetti

Every Kid's Delight!



Spaghetti? Every kid loves this food! It's often served on birthday parties and fast food restaurants! Here in the Philippines, Jolibee serves them, Mcdonalds serves them, KFC serves them, Wendys serves them - I mean like, it's pretty much very common here in our country!

Anyway, for those curious, what exactly is spaghetti? Spaghetti is a long, thin, solid, cylindrical pasta. It is a staple food of traditional Italian cuisine. Like other pasta, spaghetti is made of milled wheat and water and sometimes enriched with vitamins and minerals. Italian spaghetti is typically made from durum wheat semolina.

Usually the pasta is white because refined flour is used, but whole wheat flour may be added. Spaghettoni is a thicker form of spaghetti, while capellini is a very thin spaghetti.
